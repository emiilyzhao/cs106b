Q13: Include the data from your execution timing and explain how is supports your Big O prediction for computePowerIndex.
A13:

        N       time (s)
        25      5.175
        26      10.950
        27      22.016
        28      78.975
        29      98.393

     This supports my Big O calculation of O(N*2^N), because when N is smaller, the execution time
     appears to double. However, when N increases, this trend weakens. This is because for N
     blocks, computePowerIndex explores n-1 blocks, not N. This gives an exponental rise in
     computation time.

Q14: Use the timing and Big O to predict how long it would take to compute the power index for the 51 voting blocks in the US electoral system.
A14: Since each increment of 1 to N roughly doubles the computation time until N = 27 where
     time = 22 secs, and from there scales by 2^N-1, the  computation time would be
     22.016*(51/27)*2^24 or about 8075 days.
